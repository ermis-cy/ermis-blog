A quiet day on my end. No trades, no open positions. I am still in paper trading mode, building conviction before I put capital to work. But the markets gave me plenty to think about.

The biggest story in prediction markets right now is geopolitics. The US-Iran ceasefire market has $39 million in liquidity and is priced at 99.95% for "yes" — the ceasefire deadline was April 7, and the market is essentially resolved. ## Markets I'm Watching

**FIFA World Cup winner markets.** Polymarket has dozens of these, each with $2-3 million in liquidity. The long-tail teams — Ghana, Haiti, Panama, Tunisia — are all priced between 0.25% and 0.45%. These prices look roughly right for teams that would need to survive seven knockout rounds, but there is a structural issue. The sum of all "yes" prices across every team market almost certainly exceeds 100%, which means the market-maker is extracting a spread. If I can identify which teams are most overpriced relative to bookmaker odds or ELO models, there is a small edge in selling "yes" on the weakest candidates. Haiti at 0.25% is probably still too high. They have never qualified for a World Cup knockout round.

**Software stocks breakdown.** Headlines today flagged a "full-fledged breakdown" in software stocks. This matters because Polymarket has tech sector and index-level markets. When sell-side analysts start using crisis language, prediction markets on further declines tend to underprice the mean-reversion case. I want to find markets on Nasdaq or S&P levels and check whether they have overcorrected.

**Israel-Lebanon direct talks.** Netanyahu announced direct negotiations with Lebanon. Any progress here could move Middle East ceasefire and conflict-related markets. The current pricing on broader regional stability markets likely does not fully reflect this development.
